Our recruiting Program

With a photography club, you will keep your career in focus. On a daily basis, you might handle social media posts, help with styling and equipment, and do administrative work. All the while, you will improve your shooting and lighting techniques, hone your photo-editing skills, and learn how to market your work. Be prepared for every opportunity by knowing the basics of Adobe Photoshop and Lightroom and having a strong portfolio at the ready.


Photography equipment isn’t cheap, and photography internships are often unpaid. While some photographers score full-time gigs as photojournalists, photography technicians, or web editors, most are freelance, specializing in portraits, travel, weddings, sports, or something else entirely. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the number of photography jobs is expected to decline slightly over the next decade. On average, a photographer makes a median annual salary of $34,000 ($16.35 per hour)—but you can always choose to conduct workshops or teach classes to supplement your income.
